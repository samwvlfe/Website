Float Theme
My first plan of action with this entire project is to allign my vision with the overall theme of Homecoming. This year's theme is "110%", descibed HERE by Kellan Fenegan, the 2024 Tigerama director and a good freind of mine. Making the design along the lines of the theme can make or break the float in the judges eyes. 3 years in a row I have been a part of a float that (to me) looked a lot better but we placed second due to theme allignment. I plan to break that streak and take home first place this year.
Initial Sketch
The underlying theme is Rock and Roll. I figured we make our design based on "Hard Rock Cafe," but changed to "Howard's Rock Cafe" to make it Clemson oriented. Howards Rock is a massive part of our football program. It sits at the top of the Hill at our Stadium, and before every home game all the players and coaches run by and rub/tap it for luck and commemoration to the famous coach. You can find more info HERE. Using the staple guitar from the Hard Rock logo, I plan to make that the focal point of our float, as it will be spinning on the Y-axis, displaying different front and back designs.
Final Design
After meeting with my Homecoming team and discussing the art work that was going to be on it, I went into photoshop and made this mock up. I think it came out pretty good. It features 3 moving parts, 1. The guitar rotating. 2. A circle rotating behind the float with a cutout to show one of the multiple logos the full circle has on it at a time. 3. a 'Tiger Rag' record spinning.


This is the circle with all of the logos that will be rotating behind the float itself. It has all of the old logos in order of when they were established. The cutout will allow one logo to be visible at a time.
Pole Placement
These floats are built from frames that are bolted to telephone poles. This is the form that I sent in that shows exactly where our poles go to support our design. I was very careful in choosing where the poles go to ensure the moving parts have somewhere to be bolted to and we have enough room in the middle for the guitar to rotate freely.
